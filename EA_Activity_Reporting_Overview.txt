EA ACTIVITY REPORTING — FUNCTION OVERVIEW
==========================================


WHAT IT DOES
------------
The EA Activity Report tab scans your Obsidian meeting notes from the past
quarter and generates a structured activity table formatted for Salesforce
EA Engagement entry. It reads your notes, identifies reportable CSM
activities, classifies each one by Type and Subtype, and outputs a
Markdown table ready to copy into SFDC.


HOW TO USE IT
-------------
1. Go to the "EA Activity" tab in the app header.
2. Select the account folder you want to report on (e.g. "L3Harris",
   "Northrop Grumman", "Frontgrade").
3. Click "Scan Folder" — the app loads all notes dated within the past
   3 months.
4. (Optional) Review the keyword scrub panel. Any lines flagged as
   belonging to other accounts are removed before sending to Claude.
   Check a line to keep it if it was flagged incorrectly.
5. Choose a model (Haiku = faster/cheaper, Sonnet = better quality).
6. Click "Generate EA Activity Report."
7. The table streams in live. When complete, click "Save to Obsidian"
   to write the file to your vault, or "Copy" to copy the Markdown.


WHAT IT OUTPUTS
---------------
A Markdown table with one row per reportable activity:

  | Account | Event Date | Engagement Title | Type | Subtype | Comments |

- Account         — the account name (from the folder you selected)
- Event Date      — pulled from the ### YYYY-MM-DD heading of the source note
- Engagement Title — short descriptive name matching SFDC style
- Type            — one of 6 EA Engagement Types (see taxonomy below)
- Subtype         — specific subtype within that Type
- Comments        — up to 1,000 characters; executive-facing, CSM as subject


WHAT IT INCLUDES / EXCLUDES
----------------------------
INCLUDED:
- Meetings with customers, EA Admins, or stakeholders
- Internal NI sessions that produced a concrete decision or outcome
- User group sessions, demo days, case study work
- Training plan sessions, newsletter sends, campaign activities
- Escalations and risk management activities

EXCLUDED:
- Routine emails or calendar holds with no outcome
- Purely logistical check-ins with no substance
- Duplicate rows for the same session across multiple notes
- Content from other accounts (scrubbed before sending to Claude)


EA ENGAGEMENT TYPE TAXONOMY (SFDC VALUES)
------------------------------------------
Types and subtypes must exactly match SFDC dropdown values.

TYPE: Entitlement Awareness & Promotion
  Subtypes: Digital Campaign/Promotion | MidTerm Reviews | Newsletters |
            Shared Space Set-up/Update | Training/Support Plans |
            Training/Support Webinar | Other

TYPE: Internal Alignment & Collaboration
  (NI-internal only — no customer present)
  Subtypes: Account Planning | Account Team Kick-Off | Product Feedback | Other

TYPE: Onboarding & Kick-Off
  (EA Admins = customer-side IT contacts who manage the EA, e.g. at
   Frontgrade, LM, NGC, L3Harris — NOT NI employees)
  Subtypes: EA Admin Onboarding | EA End-User Kick-Off | Other

TYPE: Strategic Relationship Management
  (EA Admin Syncs = meetings with customer-side EA Admins, not NI staff)
  Subtypes: EA Admin Sync | Escalation/Risk Management | QBRs/EBRs |
            Roadmap Review | SLE Governance | Other

TYPE: User Groups
  (subtype depends on who led the session)
  Subtypes: Demo Days (NI-led) | User Group (customer-led) | Other

TYPE: Value Realization & Success Stories
  Subtypes: Case Study | Customer Testimonial | Outcome Review |
            SLE ROI Review | Other

TYPE: Other
  (only if none of the above truly fit)


PRIVACY / SANITIZATION
-----------------------
Before sending notes to Claude:
- Names and account references in your glossary are replaced with
  pseudonyms (reversed in the output)
- Lines containing keywords tied to other accounts are scrubbed
- You can override scrubbed lines by checking them in the scrub panel


SAVING
------
Clicking "Save to Obsidian" writes the report as a Markdown file into
your vault inside the selected account folder, named:
  "EA Activity Report YYYY-MM-DD.md"
